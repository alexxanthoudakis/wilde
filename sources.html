Sources

The Wilde Trials International News Archive highlights North American and European news reports on the Wilde trials, and includes representative news coverage from nineteen countries.

The majority of the reports have been sourced from digitized newspaper collections. These include large open-access national collections, as well as multi-title commercial databases. Smaller digitised collections devoted to individual titles have also been important sources.

To ensure a diversity of content, the archive also includes reports from non-digitised newspapers. National collections generally prioritise the digitisation of major titles, rather than specialist and foreign-language newspapers. As foreign-language newspapers are important sites of transnational cultural exchange, many of those that carried reports on the trials have been transcribed by hand at collection sites from print and microfilm sources.

With the exception of a number of American newspapers, whose curation continues, the archive includes all the reports related to the trials published by the newspapers in the collection.

News reports differ widely in format across different newspapers, and sometimes even within a newspaper. Some reports appear as stand-alone items; others tag on additional late-arriving telegraphic dispatches; yet others are aggregated with other news items in longer columns. In each case, the archive counts these as single reports.

Where reports originally appeared in columns containing unrelated news, the unrelated news has been stripped from the text. You can follow the link to the facsimile page if you wish to consult the news in context.

The text of the reports has been transcribed as accurately as possible. Original formatting is not strictly preserved, as the archive's primary interest is in the text of the report rather than its appearance, and the text-sequencing algorithm necessitates some standardisation of line breaks and crossheads.

Many hands have been involved in the transcription and correction process, acknowledged in the Contributors page. Mistakes are inevitable with this number of documents, however, and the revision process continues. It should also be noted that the London Star and the Yorkshire Evening Post have not yet gone through full revision.

Each news report includes basic metadata about the newspaper from which it was derived, as well as date of publication, word count, and source. If sourced from open-access databases, a link to the facsimile is provided. In the case of certain older databases, however, it is not possible to provide a direct link to the report.

For each news report, there is also “document match” metadata provided on the number of other matching news reports at the document level, as well as links to those reports. These are differentiated by the kinds of similarity measures used to detect matching text: either levenshtein or cosine. Levenshtein measures the number of different characters between two pieces of text. Cosine measures similarity based on word frequencies in the text.

Next to each report are match buttons. These show the number of similar paragraphs found across the collection from other newspapers. You can follow links that show side-by-side comparisons of reports with matching paragraphs.
