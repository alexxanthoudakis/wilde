Overview

The Wilde Trials International News Archive gathers international news reports covering the three trials of Anglo-Irish playwright Oscar Wilde (1854-1900).

These trials were a global media event between April and May 1895, reported by journalists interested not only in the courtroom proceedings, but also in the sensational stories that unfolded around them -- including a Piccadilly street fight and a French duel.

Lasting nearly two months, the trials triggered important debates about gender expression and sexuality, family relations, press standards, and the principle of art for art’s sake.

The archive currently includes full transcriptions of 1518 news reports (amounting to 842,439 words) that were published in newspapers from over a dozen different countries and in four languages.

Drawing on computational and machine learning methods in Digital Humanities, including custom-designed text-matching algorithms and the Google Cloud Translation API, the archive offers new opportunities for reading about Wilde’s three trials across borders and languages, and understanding a watershed event in the history of homosexuality and international journalism.

You can browse, search, and compare the reports to learn about when and how news of the trials was reported around the world.

You can also retrace how the coverage was copied, both locally and internationally, via news subscription services and telegraph communication, as well as old-fashioned cut-and-paste journalism.
