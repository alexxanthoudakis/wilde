Overview

The Wilde Trials International News Archive gathers international news reports covering the three trials of anglo-irish playwright Oscar Wilde (1854-1900). These trials were a global media event in April and May 1895 reported by journalists interested not only in the courtroom proceedings, but also in the sensational stories that unfolded around them -- including a Piccadilly street fight and a French duel. Lasting nearly two months, the trials triggered important debates about homosexuality, family relations, censorship and press standards, literary Aestheticism, and British criminal jurisprudence.

The archive currently includes full transcriptions of over 1500 news reports (amounting to 644,150 words) that were published in newspapers from over a dozen different countries and in four languages. More than a repository of digitised reports, however, the archive also enables readers to browse, compare, assemble, and analyse the reports in a number of different ways. Readers can open an individual report, compare it to similar reports, track how it was copied and translated around the world, and link it to larger news networks through access to data about the volume and range of the news coverage.

The archive was designed to give readers access to the international coverage of Wildeâ€™s three trials, but also to enable them to retrace how the news was copied around the world, via telegraphic communication, translation services, and sometimes scissors and paste journalism. Drawing on computational and machine learning methods in Digital Humanities, including custom-designed text-matching algorithms and the Google Cloud Translation API, the archive offers new opportunities for reading about the trials across borders and languages as well as new applications for analysing how they became a watershed event in the history of homosexuality and international journalism.

The archive is a work in progress, and continues to revise and develop its content. It is linked to a related project on Wilde Data & Global News.
