The Reports

The Wilde Trials International News Archive currently includes over 1500 reports published in newspapers from over a dozen different countries and in four languages. The collection highlights North American and European news reports on the Wilde trials, and continues to develop content from digital, microfilm, and print sources.

The majority of these reports have been sourced from digitized newspaper collections. These include large open-access national collections such as the BNF's Gallica's presse et revues, BNE's Hemeroteca Digital, Royal Library of Belgium's BelgicaPress, UNAM's Hemeroteca Nacional Digital de MÃ©xico, the multi-institutional Digital Library of the Caribbean, LOC's Chronicling America, and NLA's Trove. Reports have also been drawn from multi-title, cross-indexed commercial databases, such as Gale Cengage's NewsVault, the British Newspaper Archive, and Readex's and the Center for Research Libraries' World Newspaper Archive. Local collections and databases devoted to individual titles have also been important sources.

The archive also includes reports from non-digitised newspapers. National collections generally prioritise the digitisation of major titles, rather than specialist and foreign-language newspapers. As foreign-language newspapers are important sites for transnational cultural exchange and an important part of the archive, many of those that carried reports on the trials have been transcribed by hand at collection site from print and microfilm sources.

Each news report includes basic metadata about the newspaper from which it was derived, as well as date of publication, word count, and source. If sourced from open-access databases, a link to the facsimile is provided. In the case of certain older databases, however, it is not possible to provide a direct link to the report. For each news report, there is also metadata provided on the number of other news reports it matches at the document level, as well as links to those reports.

The archive includes all news reports on the trials that could be found within a newspaper and which were published roughly between April and May 1895. A number of American newspapers are an exception: in many cases, only reports on Wilde's first libel trial have thus far been gathered.

The format for these reports differs widely across newspapers, and sometimes even within a newspaper, raising questions about the ontological status of a news report. Some reports appear as stand-alone items; others appear as stand-alone items, but tag on additional late-arriving telegraphic dispatches; yet others are aggregated with other news items in longer columns. In each case, the archive counts these as single reports. Where reports originally appeared in columns containing unrelated news, the unrelated news has been stripped from the digitised text. Readers can follow the link to the facsimile page if they wish to consult the news in context.

The text of the reports has been transcribed as accurately as possible. Original formatting is not strictly preserved, as the archive's primary interest is in the text of the report rather than its appearance, and the text-sequencing algorithm necessitates some standardisation of line breaks and crossheads.

Many hands have been involved in the transcription and correction process, acknowledged in the Contributors page. Mistakes are inevitable with this number of documents, however, and the revision process continues. It should also be noted that the London Star and the Yorkshire Evening Post have not yet gone through full revision.
